The goal of this lab page is to collect and display the insights gained from the AA1 – Digestive System Simulation exercise. The importance of the digestive system is rooted in its responsibility in breaking down ingested food, undergoing digestion, converting it into crucial nutrients like proteins and carbohydrates, and absorbing these nutrients. The simulation lab exercise enriches our understanding by mimicking the various stages of the digestive process, starting from the intake of food to its eventual elimination.

Section 1 - Stages of Food Processing



       

               

              Q1: What is being simulated by putting bananas and crackers in the zip lock?

            This simulation of placing bananas and crackers in the Ziplock closely mirrors the first phase of the digestive process. The food is ingested and
            stored soon in the stomach. This helps the students in visualizing and engaging with the concept of the stomach as a reservoir.

              Q2: What is being simulated by the addition of water?

            The addition of water serves as the representation of the saliva, initiating the start of mechanical digestion by moistening the food. This vividly
            illustrated the role of saliva in breaking down complex substances.

              Q3: What does the zip-locked bag represent?

            The stomach is represented with the zip-locked in this process. Much like the stomach, the zip-lock encapsulates the undigested food.

              Q4: What does crushing the food simulate?

            Crushing of the food represents the beginning of digestion in our stomachs. This is the visual representation of how food breaks down
            into smaller bits for the subsequent steps in the process

              Q5: What is being simulated by the addition of orange juice?

            Adding the orange juice in this laboratory exercise represents the simulation of the enzymes during the first part of the process, offering
            visual representation of the natural chemicals in our stomach

              Q6: What do you think are the possible biomolecule components of the food inside?

            Biochemistry-wise, we can say that the elements that are found inside a food are the carbohydrates, proteins, fats and vital nutrients
            that contribute to our overall health.

              Q7: What is being simulated by the liquid flowing out of the stocking?

            The flowing out of liquid from the stocking creates a dynamic representation of the absorption process. The nutrients will now assimilate
            into the bloodstream, preparing for the distribution throughout the body.

              Q8: What does the tray represent?

            The tray represents either the circulatory systems (bloodstream or lymphatic channels) transferring nutrients gained from digestion
            or the organism itself receiving these nutrients.

              Q9: What is left inside the stocking?

            The residual, undigested food or the partially solidified content stored within the stockings or the gastrointestinal tract. This residue
            potentially comprises the substances needed for excretion.

              Q10: What is being simulated by pushing the partially solidified content?

            The propulsion of the partially solidified substance emulates the process of eliminating undigested food through the anus.

Section 3: Simulation of Bile Action


              Q13: Explain the role of bile in digestion. Is it involved in chemical or mechanical digestion?

            Bile,a viscous yellow-green digestive fluid synthesized by the liver and is stored in the gallbladder, plays a pivotal role in chemical
            digestion, particularly in the breakdown of fats for utilization by the body. Additionally, it is essential for the elimination of
            substances deemed unusable by the body.
